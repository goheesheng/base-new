# Standard Founder Interview Prep

Likely questions in the standard Base Batches interview, with how to answer them.

## What selectors are testing

- Can you ship?
- Do you understand your user?
- Is your "Why Base" real?
- Are you investable?

Most questions are variations of these. Answer in 60–90 seconds, with one concrete example, not abstractions.

## Likely questions

### About the product
1. **Walk me through what you built. Pretend I'm a target user.**
   *Demo the actual product, not slides. If you can't demo live, share a screen recording.*
2. **What's the riskiest assumption you're making, and how have you tested it?**
   *Have a specific test. "We assumed X, ran Y, learned Z."*
3. **Who is the first user you talked to?**
   *Specific person, specific quote. "Sarah, designer at agency Z, said quote."*
4. **What's not built yet that needs to be?**
   *Honesty. List the next 4 weeks of build. Don't claim done what isn't.*

### About the market
5. **How big is the market you're going after, and who are the next 100 users?**
   *Bottom-up. "We can reach the first 100 via Farcaster channel X, where Y people fit our ICP."*
6. **Who else is doing this, and why will you win?**
   *Name 2–3 competitors. Differentiate on a specific axis (UX, distribution, primitive used).*
7. **What's your wedge — the smallest thing you do better than anyone?**
   *One sentence. If you can't give one, the product is not focused.*

### About Base specifically
8. **Why Base?**
   *Name the primitive. "Smart Wallet passkeys for sub-10s onboarding," "Mini App distribution in Farcaster," etc. Avoid "fast and cheap."*
9. **What would break about your product on a different L2?**
   *Concrete answer. If nothing meaningfully breaks, expect skepticism.*
10. **Have you talked to anyone at Base/Coinbase about what you're building?**
   *Optional but a strong signal. Mention any conversations.*

### About the team
11. **Why this team, why now?**
   *Skin-in-the-game story. Prior shipping. Why this problem hits home.*
12. **What have you shipped together before?**
   *Specific projects. Links.*
13. **If we said no, what would you do?**
   *Strong answer: "Keep building, here's our next milestone." Weak: "Apply to other programs."*

### About metrics & ask
14. **What metric will you optimize during the program?**
   *One metric. Example: "Daily active wallets connected to our Mini App, target 200 by week 4."*
15. **What do you want from Base Batches besides the grant?**
   *Specific introductions, design partners, technical reviewers. Don't say "exposure."*

## Drafting workflow

For each question above:
1. Pull the answer from `.basestack/build-context.md` if available.
2. Write a 60–90 second answer (≈ 150–220 words spoken).
3. Stress-test: would a skeptical investor accept this? If not, rewrite.
4. Practice out loud once. Many strong written answers fall apart spoken.

## Anti-patterns

- "We're working on…" (says nothing). Replace with what's shipped.
- "Eventually we'll…" Don't pitch the roadmap; pitch this week.
- Listing six features. Pick one. Demo it.
- Reading from notes. Bullet points only.
- Inflating numbers. SME will catch it later.
